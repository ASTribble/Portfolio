About Me

I am a life-long builder of things living in Portland, Oregon.

I've built props for theaters, instruments for musicians, sets for puppets, and a pizza oven in my backyard.

My love of building in the real world led me to wonder about the virtual world.

It turns out I love that, too!

Currently, I work with a Javascript full-stack, and I have a preference for React on the front-end.

However, one thing I've learned over the years is that projects go more smoothly when I use the right tool... so I'm always excited to expand my tool-chest. I'm also ready to help others when they ask, because I never know when I'll need a hand.

Hopefully, I'll get to build something with you!

Projects

Centsible is an investment teaching application. It was built by five people, in three weeks, with an agile methodology.

I was Centsible's design-lead, so after the initial components were in place I spent most of my time on styling.

I also had a large role in the planning of the project - I created the team wireframes and worked with our project manager to break them into actionable stories in Clubhouse.

Centsible used React and Redux on the front end, Node.js for our server, and a Mongo database. Continuous integration was handeled with TravisCI, and Mocha, Chai, Enzyme, and Jest were used for testing. Workflow was managed with Clubhouse.io and Github, and the wireframes were built on moqups.com.

Git Better was built with React and Redux on the front-end, Node.js with Express on the back, and everything is stored in a MongoDB database with a Mongoose interface.

I love to pub sing. There is nothing quite like a rousing chorus of "Cousin Jack." However, sometimes John isn't at the pub to lead that particular song, and while I know the tune, I don't know the words. Also, I'm pretty busy these days and don't get to sings that often...and it would be nice to remind myself of some words before I have to sing them in front of people. This is what Song Cellar was created for. It's a place to store song lyrics for quick reference.

Song Cellar's front-end was built with HTML, CSS, and Javascript utilizing jQuery for DOM manipulation. The back-end uses Node.js with Express, and the data is stored in a MongoDB with Mongoose models and methods. Mocha and Chai were used for testing, and Travis CI for contiuous integration.
